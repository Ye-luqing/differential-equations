  \begin{theorem}[Weierstrass's M-Test]
    Let $\{y_m(x)\}$ be a sequence of functions with $|y_m(x)|\leq
    M_m$ for all $x$ in $[\alpha,\beta]$ with
    $\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}M_m<\infty$.Then $\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}y_m(x)$
    converges uniformly in $[\alpha,\beta]$ to a unique function
    $y(x)$.
  \end{theorem}
  \begin{proof}
    Very simple indeed.Still,I'd like to write my proof down.In order
    to prove that $\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}y_m(x)$ converges uniformly to
    $y(x)$ in $[\alpha,\beta]$,we only need to prove that for any
    given positive real number $\va$,there exists a positive integer
    $N$ such that for all $m>n>N$,and \textbf{for all} $x\in [\alpha,\beta]$,we have 
$$
|\sum_{i=n}^m y_i(x)|<\va.
$$
In order to prove it,we just need to prove that
$$
\sum_{i=n}^m|y_i(x)|<\va.
$$
We just need to prove that
$$
\sum_{i=n}^mM_i<\va.
$$
From $\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}M_m$,we know this is true.
  \end{proof}
